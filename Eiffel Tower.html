The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world, stands proudly in Paris, France. It was built for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World’s Fair), an event celebrating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower was originally criticized by artists and intellectuals who thought it looked strange and unnecessary. However, after its completion, it quickly became a symbol of modern engineering and a beloved icon of France.

Standing at 324 meters tall, the Eiffel Tower was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 40 years. It is made of iron and consists of three main levels accessible by elevators or stairs. The tower’s design is both elegant and extremely strong, capable of withstanding powerful winds thanks to its open lattice structure.

At night, the Eiffel Tower lights up the Paris sky with thousands of sparkling lights, making it one of the most photographed monuments on Earth. Today, it welcomes over 7 million visitors each year, offering breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. More than just a tourist attraction, the Eiffel Tower stands as a symbol of innovation, creativity, and the cultural beauty of Paris.
